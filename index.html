Code Quantum 2021

  • About Us

    Association of Computing - Women's Chapter at the University of Texas at San Antonio is a non-profit organization dedicated to building a network and community of women and minorities! Our organization is focused on encouraging and supporting our members in computing through professional development, social network, and academic initiatives.

    ACM-W is an all-inclusive organization focused on tailoring its activities towards minorities. ACM-W aims to create an engaging academic, professional, and social network for women and minorities in technology. Our purpose is to connect students with leaders and encourage them to pursue career opportunities in computing fields and to mentor for academic and professional success.

  • What is CodeQuantum?

    CodeQuantum is San Antonio’s first 24 hour hackathon geared towards femininity! Organized by the ACM-W chapter at UTSA, CodeQuantum is dedicated to creating a safe hacking space for those who are feminine, non-binary, and allies in the tech field.

The F.A.Q.

Who can attend?

CodeQuantum is free to attend and open to those who are 0 and 1 or in between! You must be a UTSA student over the age of 18. We also welcome students that have graduated in the past year!

When is CodeQuantum happening?

Hacking will be from 11am October 23rd to 11am October 24th, 2021.

Where will CodeQuantum be held?

CodeQuantum will be held at The University of Texas at San Antonio. Specific room locations will be shared at registration time.

How big are teams?

Each team can contain up to 5 students, but you are more than welcome to work alone. We do encourage every hacker to collaborate with the other great minds at CodeQuantum!

What if I am not part of the targeted hackers?

Each team can contain up to 5 students, but you are more than welcome to work alone. We do encourage every hacker to collaborate with the other great minds at CodeQuantum!
